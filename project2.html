Kindergarten Playground | Prototype

This group project was designed to as a assignment for a course called "Information Beyond Screens". The project is a design and iterative development of an interactive spinning carousel prototype, created within the themes of quantified self and street. The initial goal was to create some type of exercise equipment that generated electricity in a battery and used the stored energy to charge mobile devices. This idea would align with the theme quantified self and the area street assigned for our project.

Design Process

Iteration 1: Our initial concept emerged from a desire to merge physical activity with sustainable energy production. We envisioned installing exercise equipment, specifically a stationary exercise bike, in public parks. The bike would be equipped with a generator to convert the mechanical energy from pedaling into electrical energy, which would then be stored in an adjacent battery unit. This setup would serve as a charging station for mobile devices, incentivizing users to engage in physical activity while contributing to renewable energy efforts.
Iteration 2: Acknowledging the limitations of our initial idea, we shifted our focus toward creating an interactive experience for children. We conceptualized a spinning device—a carousel—that would encourage physical activity through play. The initial design was a small apparatus intended for one child to stand and spin independently. This pivot aimed to foster physical engagement in a manner that was both intuitive and enjoyable for younger users.
Iteration 3: In response to the feedback we got from the teacher and other students, we expanded the carousel's design to accommodate multiple children simultaneously. The larger carousel promoted social interaction, allowing children to play together, fostering teamwork, and enhancing the overall enjoyment of the experience. We recognized that play is not only about physical activity but also about social development and learning to interact with others.
Iteration 4: To enrich the user experience and deepen engagement, we decided to integrate multisensory feedback into the carousel. We introduced auditory elements by incorporating the popular children's song "Baby Shark." The music would play automatically when the carousel started spinning, with the tempo increasing in correlation with the speed of rotation. This feature provided immediate, intuitive feedback, encouraging children to spin faster to hear the song accelerate.

My Contributions

  • Collaborated in designing an interactive spinning carousel focused on implicit and embodied interaction
  • Contributed to developing multisensory feedback mechanisms using sound and light
  • Integrated features inspired by quantified self, such as rotation counters and heat sensors
  • Ensured safety and usability through iterative design critiques and workshops
  • Enhanced engagement by implementing automatic activation and scaling feedback intensity

Outcome

The final product is an interactive spinning carousel designed to encourage physical activity and engagement in public play spaces. Key features include automatic activation, multisensory feedback with sound and light, and quantified self-inspired elements such as a rotation counter and heat sensors. The carousel successfully integrates implicit and embodied interaction principles, creating a seamless, engaging experience for children while addressing safety and educational aspects through iterative user-centered design.
